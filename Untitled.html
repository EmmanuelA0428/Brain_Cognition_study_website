Key Points:

  • Each eye sees both visual fields (left and right)
  • The lens flips the image, so the left side of what you see (left visual field) hits the right side of each retina
  • Nasal retina fibers cross at the optic chiasm
  • Temporal retina fibers stay on the same side
  • All information from the right visual field ends up in the left visual cortex
